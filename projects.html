Stathub

About This Project

The goal of Stathub is to predict the Premier League (english soccer league) using machine learning. The data used in this model includes previous years league positions (where each team finished) in addition to financial data (total value of each team) for the league. This data will take into consideration the performance of clubs in addition to their financial might. The data is collected through webscraping Transfermarkt.com, a website that collects data of soccer teams across the world in addition to evaluating a transfer market value for players. For example, Mbappe, a young forward taking Europe by storm is one of the most valuable players in the world at 180 million euros. Messi, a much older player of similar or better quality to Mbappe, is worth 50 million. The stark contrast is due to younger players being worth more due to their long term potential in the team.

Iteration One

Iteration one consists of webscraping data and creating a machine learning model. This model takes in one input, which is relative market value, and the output is the predicted number of points.

Iteration Two

The second iteration also has the same webscraping script. However, it takes two inputs, which are GD and relative market value and the output is the league table (points per team).

Next Steps

Although performance and financial standing are great indicators of how a team will perform, there are still many other factors that affect a team's performance such as injuries, the manager, and more. In the future, I'd like to improve this model by taking into consideration more factors in my model such as xG, injury status, and more. This model can be improved to predict the outcome of each game instead of the end of the league placements.
