Stathub

About This Project

The goal of Stathub is to predict the Premier League Table using regression. The data used in this model includes previous years league positions for the teams in addition to financial data regarding their team. This data will take into consideration the performance of clubs in addition to their financial might. The data is collected through webscraping Transfermarkt.com, a website that collects data of soccer teams across the world in addition to evaluating a transfer market value for players. For example, Mbappe, a young forward taking Europe by storm is one of the most valuable players in the world at 180 million euros. Messi, a much older player of similar or better quality to Mbappe, is worth 50 million. The stark contrast is due to younger players being worth more due to their long term potential in the team.

What This Project Means to Me

As a lifelong soccer fan, combining my interests of data science and soccer is a very engaging way to create projects to help me learn. I don't expect Stathub to be a perfect prediction. Instead I want to learn the data science process in a hands on format and keep improving on what I have. I hope to get my hands dirty with more updates to the model in the future. Thanks for reading this and feel free to click on the links below to see various parts of the project.

Iteration One

Iteration one consists of webscraping data and creating a machine learning model. This model takes in one input, which is relative market value, and the output is the predicted number of points.

Next Steps

Although performance and financial standing are great indicators of how a team will perform, there are still many other factors that affect a team's performance such as injuries, the manager, and various other factors. In the future, I'd like to improve this model by taking into consideration more factors in my model such as xG, injury status, and more. This model can be improved to predict the outcome of each game instead of the end of the league placements.
